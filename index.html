About Our Project

Our project, developed through the engineering leadership program at Cornell, focuses on providing support to Second Wind Cottages, a local organization that provides homes and rehabilitat for the homeless

  • April 2018

    Brainstorming

    Our team first came together as part of the leadership retreat focused around develpoing our projects. While we all had different interests and engagements, we talked and brainstormed over the retreat to come up with many possible ideas for our project and developed a team contract. Using thism, we identified Second Wind as a good fit for our interests and reached out.

  • Summer 2018

    The Work Begins

    After meeting with Second Wind and discussing the project, we sent members to the first Build Day, a event where community members come together to build the cottages for residents. We were thrown right into the action, building walls and trusses and meeting many people from around the community. We continued attending these events throughout the summer and into the Fall semester.

  • Fall 2018

    Sustainability Work

    Once the Fall semester began and our team was all together again, we worked more on our individual projects to help improve sustainability. Through local organizations, we conducted an energy audit to enhance energy savings, and developed a larger scale sustainability plan for the organizations future. We also continued attending build days as a team, keeping the connection between our groups and helping finish off the new cottages.

  • December 2018

    Project Transition

    With the end of our leadership program, we began to wrap up our contributions to the project and looked to other groups to continue our work. We identified the Engineers for a Sustainable World (ESW) project team at Cornell as a perfect group to continue our work and keep developing sustainability plans for Second Wind. We also hosted some final events, including hosting a group of students from our class at a build day, and hosting a dinner for Second Wind Residents.
